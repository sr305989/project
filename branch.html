BRANCH


Structural support
Branches support the tree's leaves, fruits, and flowers. They also help the tree spread its leaves to gather sunlight for photosynthesis.
Transportation
Branches transport water from the soil to the leaves and food from the leaves to the rest of the tree.
Reproduction
Branches spread out the tree's reproductive structures, like flowers and fruits, increasing the likelihood of successful pollination and seed dispersal.
Habitat
Branches provide habitats for various animals, including birds, insects, and mammals.
Tree branches can also be used for:
Firewood
You can use leftover tree branches for kindling in your fireplace or fire pit.
Upcycling
You can use tree branches to create stools, garment racks, garden arbors, coffee tables, floor lamps, curtain rods, brackets, and candles.
